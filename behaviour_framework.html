Problematic Behaviour
Assessment Framework

A structured tool for prioritising intervention targets. Fixed columns reflect published data; Feasibility and Measurability can be adjusted to reflect your own context.

Impact
Environmental impact of changing the behaviour, scored 1–5. Higher means greater reduction in emissions or resource use.
Feasibility
How achievable the behaviour change is for your target population, given structural, economic, and social constraints. Editable.
Measurability
How well the behaviour can be measured in your study or intervention context. Editable.
Obj. Meas.
Objective measurement availability — whether reliable sensor or administrative data exists to track this behaviour without self-report.
Self-rep (%)
Percentage of respondents in the source survey who reported currently performing this behaviour.
Self-rep (1–5)
Self-reported behaviour score on a 1–5 scale, derived from the same survey. Higher = more respondents already doing it.
Willing (%)
Percentage willing to adopt the behaviour if prompted or supported — a proxy for intervention receptivity.
Willing (1–5)
Willingness score on a 1–5 scale. Combines stated and inferred willingness from survey data.
Diff. Income
Income-group disparity in the behaviour — how much adoption differs across socioeconomic strata. Higher = more inequitable distribution.
Total Score
Composite priority score — equal-weighted average of Impact, Feasibility, Measurability, Obj. Meas., Self-rep, Willingness, and Income difference. Updates live when you adjust sliders.
